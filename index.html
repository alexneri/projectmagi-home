// Tri-Persona Adversarial Reasoning for OSINT & GEOINT

Intelligence analysis drowns in data, starved of deliberation time.
Single-model pipelines are fast but epistemically fragile. One perspective. Correlated failure modes. Opaque confidence.

MAGI embeds deliberation into the pipeline through three agents — each operating in a distinct epistemic mode.

01   The Three Minds
PERSONA_01 // MELCHIOR
Melchior
Evidence Analyst

Grounds the analysis in verifiable data. Anchors every claim to sourced evidence. Applies inductive reasoning to surface what the data actually shows — uncoloured by pattern or precedent.

Epistemic Mode: Empirical
PERSONA_02 // BALTHASAR
Balthasar
Pattern Strategist

Operates at the systemic level. Identifies patterns, trends, and structural dynamics across intelligence signals. Applies abductive reasoning to construct competing strategic models.

Epistemic Mode: Abductive
PERSONA_03 // CASPAR
Caspar
Procedural Jurist

Evaluates the logical and procedural integrity of the analysis. Challenges leaps in reasoning, enforces epistemic standards, and produces a minority dissent when warranted.

Epistemic Mode: Deductive
02   Capabilities

Built for hostile
information environments

Designed to handle intelligence workflows where single-model confidence is a liability, not a feature.

01

OSINT Signal Analysis

Multi-source open-source intelligence processing with adversarial credibility scoring. Melchior/Balthasar pipeline cross-examines each signal before it surfaces.

02

GEOINT Interpretation

Satellite imagery and geospatial analysis workflows. Structured assessment of spatial intelligence through layered epistemic review.

03

Epistemic Divergence Detection

Genuine Divergence Rate (GDR) metric tracks whether personas produce meaningfully distinct conclusions — not just tonal variation under evidence pressure.

04

Judicial Format Output

Every analysis produces a four-section structured brief: uncertainty map, conditional recommendation, confidence declaration, and minority dissent when views diverge.

05

Calibrated Confidence

Explicit uncertainty mapping at every inference step. No opaque confidence scores — just traceable reasoning chains with annotated uncertainty at each node.

06

Open-Source Toolchain

Built on a composable, open-source pipeline. Governance documentation, evaluation design, and end-to-end workflow specs included in the research paper.

The Judicial Brief Format

Role definitions grounded in distinct epistemic modes — not attitudes — are more likely to produce genuine divergence than optimist/skeptic roles, which collapse under evidence pressure.

Every analysis concludes in a four-section structured output designed for analyst consumption.

Output Sections
  • Uncertainty map — explicit confidence bounds per claim
  • Conditional recommendation — action guidance under stated assumptions
  • Confidence declaration — joint and individual credibility scores
  • Minority dissent — Caspar's override when logic breaks
